Takoyaki


Image source: i am a food blog

Description

Takoyaki is a popular street food from Japan. It has since become famous in different countries.

Typically, it is made of wheat flour, eggs, and dashi with diced octopus, tempura scraps, and green onion. The mixture is then cooked in a special takoyaki pan, which is designed to make the snack in the shape of small round balls.

Ingredients and tool

  • 2 cups of flour
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup of dashi (Japanese fish stock) or water
  • 1/2 cup of diced octopus
  • 1/4 cup of tempura scraps
  • 2 green onions, finely chopped
  • Takoyaki sauce, mayonnaise and aonori (dried seaweed flakes) and katsuobushi (dried bonito flakes) for topping
  • Takoyaki pan

Steps

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the flour, eggs, and dashi or water to make the batter. Mix until smooth.
  2. Add the diced octopus, tempura scraps, and green onions to the batter and mix well.
  3. Heat the takoyaki pan over medium-high heat. Once hot, brush the cups with some oil.
  4. Pour the batter into the cups, filling them about 3/4 full.
  5. Use a skewer or the end of a chopstick to poke a small hole in the center of each ball.
  6. Once the edges start to firm up, use the skewers or chopsticks to flip the balls over in the pan.
  7. Continue cooking until the balls are golden brown and cooked through.
  8. Remove the takoyaki from the pan and place them on a plate.
  9. Brush with takoyaki sauce and mayonnaise, and then top with aonori and katsuobushi.
  10. Serve immediately and enjoy!
Note: This recipe is based on the traditional Takoyaki recipe, you can change the ingredients according to your preference.
